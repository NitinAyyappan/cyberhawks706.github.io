About

Arrowhead High School Robotics Team

Team History

The Arrowhead Robotics Team was founded in 2001, originally under the name APE Robotics (Arrowhead Price Engineering Robotics). In 2003, the team changed its name to the Cyberhawks.

Over the years, the team has achieved multiple awards, including appearances at regional and world championships, the Motorola Quality Award, Xerox Creativity Award, General Motors Industrial Design Award, Rockwell Automation Innovation in Control Award, and the FIRST Dean’s List Finalist Award.

Beyond awards, the team actively engages with the community, promoting STEM education and leadership.

Sub-teams

Our team is organized into three main sub-teams: Build, Programming, and Business.

Build Team

Focused on design and construction of the competition robot. Members learn CAD and Inventor for professional-level drawings and gain hands-on experience operating shop tools.

Programming Team

Members write Java code for the robot and handle wiring. During pre-season, they learn coding basics; during build season, they gain hands-on experience coding and system integration.

Business Team

Manages finances, sponsors, and documentation. Members track income and expenses, develop communication skills, and handle award submissions like the Impact Award.
